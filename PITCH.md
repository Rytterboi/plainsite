# The Business Case — plainsite

## What we built

plainsite is a dead simple web hosting service for small local businesses.
No WordPress. No plugins. No databases. No attack surface. Just clean, fast,
professional websites that run forever without anyone touching them.

The client experience is this: they send a WhatsApp message with what they want
changed. It's live the same day. That's the entire relationship.

---

## The problem it solves

The small business website industry is broken in a very specific way.

Most local businesses — dentists, lawyers, accountants, physiotherapists,
psychologists — are sitting on a WordPress site that was built 5-10 years ago,
cost them 15.000 DKK to set up, and now costs 2-3.000 DKK/month to "maintain."
That maintenance mostly means someone logs in once a quarter to click Update Plugins.

These sites are slow. They look outdated. And they get hacked — regularly.
We started building this after a psychology practice's site was compromised and
started serving pornographic content to their clients. They had no idea for weeks.

WordPress powers 43% of the internet and is by far the most-attacked platform
online. It's not a question of if a WordPress site gets hacked — it's when.

---

## Why our approach is different

We don't use WordPress. We don't use a database. We don't have a CMS login page.
There is genuinely nothing to attack.

The result:

- **Loads instantly** — faster than almost anything at this price point. Google notices. Clients notice.
- **Never gets hacked** — structurally impossible. No database to dump, no login to brute-force, no plugins to exploit.
- **Never breaks** — no plugin updates, no PHP version drift, no compatibility nightmares. It just runs.
- **Changes same day** — client sends a message, we handle it in minutes.

---

## The offer

- **Free for 3 months** — we rebuild their site at no cost, they compare. Zero risk.
- **500-1.000 DKK/month** after that — cheaper than what they're paying now.
- **No contract, cancel anytime** — we don't trap clients. They stay because the service is good.
- **No lock-in on their content** — there's no database. Their content is their website. They can leave whenever and take everything with them. We're confident enough in the product that we don't need to hold anyone hostage.

---

## The economics

Each client takes half a day to set up and 15-30 minutes per month to maintain
(most months zero — they just run). Hosting costs are cents. The margin is ~95%.

At 35-40 clients at 1.000 DKK/month that's a full-time salary run entirely on the side.
The first 10 clients is a serious secondary income.

them are terrible — outdated WordPress installs, broken mobile layouts, slow,
embarrassing. They are exactly the target client for plainsite.

LeagueOS is already building a direct relationship with these clubs. When a club
signs up for the league management platform, the conversation about their website
is a natural next step. They already trust the product, they already know the team.

**"By the way — your website is also pretty bad. We can fix that for 500 DKK/month,
free for the first 3 months, cancel whenever."**

That's an upsell on a warm lead to a customer who already has the problem and
already trusts you. It doesn't get easier than that.

The two products reinforce each other:
- LeagueOS brings the relationship
- plainsite monetises the relationship further with almost no extra work
- A bowling club using both is deeply embedded in the ecosystem

## Why now

Three months ago this wasn't viable as a one-person operation. The bottleneck
was always: client wants a change, developer has to context-switch, find the right
file, make the edit, test it, deploy. 30 minutes per small change.

With Claude that's 5 minutes. The entire codebase fits in a single AI context window.
Any change is a one-shot edit, committed and live in under 60 seconds via Railway.

The leverage ratio that makes this work as a side business simply didn't exist before.
It does now. And most people haven't noticed yet.
